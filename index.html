An intro to Costa Rica

Costa Rica, a small country located in Central America, is renowned for its stunning biodiversity, eco-tourism, and commitment to conservation. It is bordered by Nicaragua to the north, Panama to the south, the Pacific Ocean to the west, and the Caribbean Sea to the east. Despite its size, Costa Rica is home to a vast array of ecosystems, from tropical rainforests and cloud forests to beautiful beaches and volcanic mountains.

The country has a rich history and culture, with a strong emphasis on peace, democracy, and environmental sustainability. Costa Rica abolished its military in 1949 and has since invested heavily in education, healthcare, and environmental protection. This focus on conservation has made it a leader in eco-tourism, attracting visitors from all over the world who come to explore its national parks, wildlife, and sustainable tourism practices.

Costa Rica is famous for its "Pura Vida" lifestyle, which translates to "Pure Life" and reflects the country's laid-back and optimistic approach to life. Its people, known as "Ticos," are known for their friendliness and hospitality.

With an excellent climate, Costa Rica offers a year-round destination for travelers. Whether you're interested in hiking through rainforests, zip-lining, surfing world-class waves, or relaxing in hot springs near active volcanoes, Costa Rica offers something for every type of traveler.
